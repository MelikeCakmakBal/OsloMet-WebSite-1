Hello, I will try to write an article about how this site works and how you tested your codes. I hope you like it. This site, W3C Validator, is a standard validation tool provided by W3C, the organization that sets the web standards on the internet. W3C was founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee and publishes various rules and recommendations to ensure the development and compatibility of the web. Web sites that are coded according to these rules look better on different browsers and devices, load faster, are more accessible, and are easier to find by search engines. W3C Validator checks the HTML and XHTML codes of web sites according to these standards and shows the errors, warnings, and suggestions. This way, web developers can correct and improve their codes. To use W3C Validator, you need to do the following:
- First, go to the [W3C Validator] page. - At the top of the page, choose one of the options "Validate by URI", "Validate by File Upload", or "Validate by Direct Input". These options determine where you will load your codes from.
- The "Validate by URI" option allows you to enter the address of your web site directly. This option can be used when your web site is online and accessible to the internet.
- The "Validate by File Upload" option allows you to upload an HTML or XHTML file that is saved on your computer. This option can be used when your web site is not yet published or inaccessible to the internet.
- The "Validate by Direct Input" option allows you to paste your codes directly into the text area. This option can be used when you want to test only a part or a small piece of your web site.
- Depending on the option you chose, enter or upload the address, file, or codes of your web site to the required area.
- At the bottom of the page, open the "More Options" section and select the character encoding, document type, and other settings of your codes. These settings ensure that your codes are recognized and checked correctly. If you are not sure about these settings, you can use the default values.
- Click the "Check" button to start checking your codes.
- After a few seconds, the check result page will open. On this page, you can see whether your codes are valid or not, how many errors and warnings are found, which line and column the errors are on, the reasons for the errors, and how they can be fixed. You can also see the source code, the cleaned version, the outline view, and other options of your codes.
- Carefully examine the errors and warnings and make the necessary changes to correct your codes.
After correcting, click the "Revalidate" button to check again. Repeat this process until your codes are completely valid. W3C Validator is a useful and reliable tool to check the compliance of web sites with the standards. Testing your codes with this tool will increase the quality and performance of your web site. Therefore, as web developers, we need to use W3C Validator regularly.
